Comprehensive hashtag research, selection, and performance tracking. Load before creating content or running hashtag research.

## Hashtag Tiers

Every hashtag falls into one of five tiers. A well-constructed hashtag set mixes tiers for maximum discoverability.

### Tier 1: Branded Hashtags
Hashtags unique to your brand, product, or campaign.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Examples | `#YourBrandName`, `#YourCampaignName`, `#YourProductName` |
| Volume | Low (you create the volume) |
| Competition | None (you own it) |
| Purpose | Brand tracking, UGC collection, campaign measurement |
| Use when | Every post (1 branded hashtag per post) |

### Tier 2: Industry Hashtags
Broad hashtags that define your industry or space.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Examples | `#SaaS`, `#ContentMarketing`, `#B2BMarketing`, `#Ecommerce` |
| Volume | High (100K-10M+ posts) |
| Competition | Very high |
| Purpose | Category association, reaching broad industry audience |
| Use when | 1-2 per post. These put you in the conversation but you won't rank. |

### Tier 3: Niche Hashtags
Specific to your sub-niche. Smaller volume but higher relevance.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Examples | `#LinkedInGrowth`, `#ColdEmailTips`, `#SaaSFounder`, `#PLGStrategy` |
| Volume | Medium (10K-100K posts) |
| Competition | Moderate |
| Purpose | Reaching your specific target audience |
| Use when | 2-3 per post. These are your discovery engine. |

### Tier 4: Trending Hashtags
Time-sensitive hashtags tied to current events, challenges, or viral topics.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Examples | `#2026Predictions`, `#TechLayoffs`, platform-specific trending tags |
| Volume | Variable (spikes and fades) |
| Competition | High during peak, drops fast |
| Purpose | Riding a wave for short-term visibility |
| Use when | Only when genuinely relevant to your content. Never force a trending tag. |

### Tier 5: Community Hashtags
Hashtags created by or for specific communities.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Examples | `#BuildInPublic`, `#WritingCommunity`, `#MarketingTwitter`, `#CreatorEconomy` |
| Volume | Medium (10K-500K posts) |
| Competition | Moderate but engaged |
| Purpose | Joining active communities, getting engagement from real humans |
| Use when | 1-2 per post when content genuinely fits the community |

## Per-Platform Limits and Best Practices

### LinkedIn

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | 30 |
| Recommended count | 3-5 |
| Placement | Bottom of post, after a line break |
| Format | `#HashtagCase` (capitalize each word) |
| Optimal mix | 1 branded + 1 industry + 2-3 niche |
| What to avoid | More than 5 (looks spammy), hashtags in the body text, irrelevant tags |

**Algorithm note:** LinkedIn's algorithm uses hashtags to categorize content and show it to users who follow those hashtags. More hashtags does NOT equal more reach. 3-5 well-chosen hashtags outperform 10+ random ones.

### Twitter/X

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | No hard limit (but included in character count) |
| Recommended count | 0-2 |
| Placement | End of tweet, or woven naturally into text |
| Format | `#lowercase` or `#CamelCase` |
| Optimal mix | 0-1 trending + 0-1 niche |
| What to avoid | More than 2 (engagement drops), hashtag-only tweets, spaces in hashtags |

**Algorithm note:** Twitter's algorithm has de-emphasized hashtags over time. Content quality and engagement matter far more. Use hashtags only when they add genuine discoverability (events, movements).

### Instagram

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | 30 |
| Recommended count | 5-10 |
| Placement | First comment (cleaner caption) or end of caption after 5 line breaks |
| Format | `#lowercase` preferred |
| Optimal mix | 1 branded + 2 industry + 3-5 niche + 1-2 community |
| What to avoid | 30 hashtags (algorithm now penalizes max usage), irrelevant popular tags, banned hashtags |

**Algorithm note:** Instagram reduced the effectiveness of hashtags significantly in 2023-2024. They still help with categorization but are less important than Reels performance, saves, and shares. 5-10 relevant hashtags is the current sweet spot.

### TikTok

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | Included in 4,000 character caption limit |
| Recommended count | 3-5 |
| Placement | In caption |
| Format | `#lowercase` |
| Optimal mix | 1-2 trending + 2-3 niche + 0-1 branded |
| What to avoid | `#fyp` and `#foryoupage` (debunked -- no impact), irrelevant trending tags |

**Algorithm note:** TikTok uses hashtags to categorize content for the For You Page. Niche hashtags help the algorithm find your target audience faster. Trending hashtags can boost initial distribution if the content matches.

### YouTube

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | 15 tags in the tag field, 3 hashtags in title/description (first 3 appear above title) |
| Recommended count | 3-5 hashtags + 5-10 tags |
| Placement | First 3 hashtags in description (appear above video title), additional tags in tag field |
| Format | `#CapitalizedWords` |
| Optimal mix | 1 branded + 2-3 topic-specific + 1 series tag |
| What to avoid | More than 15 tags (YouTube may ignore all), misleading tags (policy violation) |

**Algorithm note:** YouTube tags and hashtags are less important than title, thumbnail, and watch time. They help with categorization but are not a primary discovery mechanism.

### Threads

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Max allowed | No documented limit |
| Recommended count | 0-1 |
| Placement | Natural placement in text |
| Format | `#lowercase` |
| What to avoid | Using hashtags as a growth strategy on Threads (platform is conversation-driven, not hashtag-driven) |

### Reddit

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Hashtags used | No. Hashtags are not a Reddit convention. |
| Alternative | Use post flair (required in many subreddits) |

## Research Methodology

### Step 1: Seed list
Start with 5-10 obvious hashtags for your niche. These are your seed hashtags.

### Step 2: Explore related tags
For each seed hashtag, find related hashtags:
- **LinkedIn:** Search the hashtag, look at what similar posts use
- **Instagram:** Search the hashtag, check "Related" suggestions
- **TikTok:** Search the hashtag, check "Related hashtags" and "Others searched for"
- **Twitter:** Search the hashtag, look at what's trending alongside it

### Step 3: Categorize by tier
Sort every hashtag you found into the 5 tiers. Aim for at least:
- 1-2 branded
- 5-10 industry
- 15-25 niche
- 5-10 community
- Track trending separately (these change constantly)

### Step 4: Check volume and competition
For each hashtag, estimate:
- **Volume:** How many posts use this tag? (Low: <10K, Medium: 10K-100K, High: 100K+)
- **Recency:** Are recent posts getting engagement, or is the tag dead?
- **Relevance:** Does the content under this tag match YOUR content?
- **Quality:** Are real people posting, or is it mostly spam/bots?

### Step 5: Build hashtag sets
Create 3-5 rotating hashtag sets per platform. Each set should follow the tier mix guidelines. Rotate sets to avoid looking repetitive.

## Hashtag Sets Per Content Pillar

Create a hashtag set for each content pillar in your strategy.

### Template

```
## Content Pillar: {pillar name}

### LinkedIn set (3-5 tags)
#BrandedTag #IndustryTag #NicheTag1 #NicheTag2 #CommunityTag

### Instagram set (5-10 tags)
#branded #industry1 #industry2 #niche1 #niche2 #niche3 #niche4 #community1 #community2

### TikTok set (3-5 tags)
#niche1 #niche2 #trending1 #branded

### Twitter/X set (1-2 tags)
#niche1 #community1
```

Create 3 variations per pillar per platform. Rotate to avoid repetition.

## Banned / Shadow-Banned Hashtag Detection

Some hashtags are banned or "shadow-banned" -- using them can reduce your reach or hide your content entirely.

### How to check
1. **Search the hashtag** on the platform
2. **Check results:** If the hashtag returns no results, limited results, or a warning message, it's likely restricted
3. **Instagram specific:** If you see "Recent posts are hidden because the community has reported some content" -- the tag is restricted
4. **TikTok specific:** If the hashtag page shows 0 views or redirects -- it's likely restricted

### Common banned categories
- Sexual or adult content terms
- Drug-related terms
- Self-harm related terms
- Harassment terms
- Some seemingly innocent terms that were co-opted (check regularly)

### Prevention
- Check every hashtag before first use
- Re-check monthly (bans change)
- Never use a hashtag you haven't personally verified
- If a post gets unusually low reach, check if any hashtag was recently banned
- Maintain a `hashtag-blocklist.md` in the workspace with known banned tags

## Performance Tracking

### Metrics to track per hashtag
| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|------------------|
| Reach contributed | How much reach came from this hashtag vs. others |
| Impressions from hashtag | Instagram Insights shows this directly |
| Engagement rate of hashtagged posts | Compare engagement of posts with vs. without specific tags |
| Follower growth from hashtag | New followers who discovered you via hashtag search |

### Tracking cadence
- **Weekly:** Review top-performing hashtags from the week's posts
- **Monthly:** Compare hashtag set performance, retire underperformers, add new ones
- **Quarterly:** Full hashtag audit -- refresh all sets, research new trends

### Hashtag performance log template
```
## Hashtag Performance -- {month}

| Hashtag | Platform | Posts used | Avg reach | Avg eng rate | Status |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| #tag1 | LinkedIn | 8 | 2,400 | 4.2% | Keep |
| #tag2 | LinkedIn | 8 | 1,100 | 1.8% | Replace |
| #tag3 | Instagram | 12 | 3,200 | 5.1% | Keep |

Replaced: #tag2 -> #tag5 (higher relevance to current pillar)
Added: #tag6 (trending in niche)
Removed: #tag4 (low relevance, declining volume)
```
