# Growth Waterfall -- SOCIAL:OS

Cascading logic for discovering and growing social audiences. Try the highest-signal source first, cascade down on exhaustion, track which sources produce the best followers. Every step feeds the next -- growth compounds.

**Override:** Users can change the waterfall order in workspace RULES.md under `## Growth waterfall overrides`. If no overrides exist, use the defaults below.

---

## Two-stage model

```
Stage 1: Discovery waterfall (where to find new audiences)
  -> Identify people and communities worth engaging
  -> Score each discovered target using audience-scoring.md
  -> Only proceed to Stage 2 for targets scoring 51+

Stage 2: Engagement waterfall (how to convert discovered audiences)
  -> Cascading engagement tactics from lightest to deepest
  -> Match depth to audience score tier
  -> Track conversion at each step
```

---

## Stage 1: Discovery waterfall

Try sources in this order. Each source has diminishing returns -- move to the next when the current source stops producing new high-scoring targets.

### 1. Competitor followers and engagers

**What:** Identify who follows, likes, comments on, and shares your competitors' content.

**How:**
- List 3-5 direct competitors and 3-5 adjacent brands
- Review comments on their top-performing posts (last 30 days)
- Note repeat engagers -- people who comment on multiple posts are high-value
- Check who shares/reposts their content with added commentary (these are thought leaders, not passive consumers)

**Platform tactics:**
- LinkedIn: review post comments and article engagement; check "People also viewed" on competitor profiles
- Twitter/X: check quote tweets, reply threads, and Lists that include competitors
- Instagram: review tagged posts and comment sections on Reels
- YouTube: check comment sections on videos and community tab engagement
- TikTok: review stitch and duet responses to competitor content

**Signal quality:** High -- these people are already interested in your space.
**Typical yield:** 20-50 high-quality targets per competitor.
**When to move on:** When you are seeing the same names repeatedly across competitors.

### 2. Hashtag communities

**What:** Find active participants in hashtags relevant to your pillars.

**How:**
- Identify 10-20 hashtags across your content pillars (check workspace PILLARS.md)
- Sort by "Recent" not "Top" to find active but not yet viral creators
- Look for people using niche hashtags (under 100K posts) -- these are more targeted than broad hashtags
- Track which hashtags produce the most relevant targets

**Platform tactics:**
- LinkedIn: search hashtag feeds; follow hashtags to see who posts consistently
- Twitter/X: use advanced search with hashtags; check trending hashtags in your niche
- Instagram: explore hashtag pages; check "Related hashtags" for deeper niches
- TikTok: search hashtags and review creators in the "Videos" tab

**Signal quality:** Medium-high -- hashtag users are self-identifying their interests.
**Typical yield:** 10-30 targets per hashtag.
**When to move on:** When hashtag communities become repetitive or too broad.

### 3. Industry conversations and trending topics

**What:** Find people actively discussing topics relevant to your brand right now.

**How:**
- Monitor industry news and identify who is commenting on it
- Search for recent posts about pain points your product/content addresses
- Check trending topics within your industry verticals
- Follow industry events and conferences -- speakers and attendees are high-value targets

**Platform tactics:**
- LinkedIn: search posts by keyword (last 7 days); check LinkedIn News comments
- Twitter/X: use advanced search for keywords + date range; monitor relevant Spaces
- Reddit: search relevant subreddits for recent discussions; sort by "Hot" and "Rising"
- YouTube: check trending videos in your category; review commenters

**Signal quality:** High -- timing is optimal; they are thinking about your topic now.
**Typical yield:** 5-20 targets per conversation.
**When to move on:** When trending topics shift. This source refreshes constantly.

### 4. Related creators' audiences

**What:** Find audiences through creators you already engage with or admire.

**How:**
- Identify 5-10 creators whose audience overlaps with yours but who are not direct competitors
- Review who engages with their content (same method as competitor analysis)
- Check their "following" list for potential targets
- Look at who they collaborate with -- second-degree connections are valuable

**Platform tactics:**
- LinkedIn: check mutual connections; review "Featured" sections for collaboration partners
- Twitter/X: check who they follow and interact with most; review their Lists
- Instagram: check tagged accounts and collaboration posts
- YouTube: review featured channels and video collaborators

**Signal quality:** Medium-high -- pre-filtered by a creator with audience overlap.
**Typical yield:** 15-40 targets per related creator.
**When to move on:** When you exhaust the immediate network and start hitting third-degree connections.

### 5. Community platforms (Reddit, Discord, Slack)

**What:** Find engaged audiences in community platforms where deeper conversations happen.

**How:**
- Score communities using the community scoring model in audience-scoring.md
- Join communities scoring 51+
- Identify the most active and helpful members -- these are natural leaders
- Note frequent question-askers -- they have pain points you might address
- Look for "who should I follow" threads -- goldmine for finding creators

**Platform tactics:**
- Reddit: sort subreddits by subscriber count and activity; check sidebar for related subreddits; identify top contributors via post history
- Discord: search server directories (Disboard, Discord.me); check channel activity before engaging
- Slack: find communities via Slofile, community directories, or industry lists; check #introductions for new member volume
- Facebook Groups: search by keyword; filter by activity level; check admin responsiveness

**Signal quality:** Medium -- requires more effort to convert but produces deeply engaged followers.
**Typical yield:** 10-25 targets per community.
**When to move on:** When you are an active, recognized member and have identified all high-value participants.

### 6. Newsletter cross-promotion

**What:** Find audiences through newsletter creators in adjacent spaces.

**How:**
- Identify 10-20 newsletters in your space (use Beehiiv Boost, Substack recommendations, or manual search)
- Check who runs them -- these creators often have social presence too
- Look for newsletters that feature other creators -- potential swap partners
- Review newsletter recommendation sections for audience overlap signals

**Tactics:**
- Subscribe to target newsletters and engage with their content on social
- Check if they have a "Recommended by" or "Also subscribe to" list
- Look for newsletters with "Share" incentives -- their audience is already primed to spread content
- Cross-reference newsletter creators with their social profiles for scoring

**Signal quality:** Medium -- newsletter subscribers are high-intent readers.
**Typical yield:** 5-15 targets per newsletter ecosystem.
**When to move on:** When you have mapped the relevant newsletter landscape.

### 7. Podcast and event audiences

**What:** Find audiences through podcast guests, hosts, and event speakers/attendees.

**How:**
- Identify podcasts in your niche (search Apple Podcasts, Spotify, or Listen Notes)
- Note guests and hosts -- they are content creators with established audiences
- Check upcoming industry events, conferences, and webinars
- Review speaker lists and attendee discussions on social

**Tactics:**
- Follow podcast guests on social before/after their episode airs
- Engage with event hashtags during live events
- Connect with speakers in the days surrounding an event (timing score boost)
- Review event recap posts for engaged attendees

**Signal quality:** Medium -- podcasts and events filter for engaged, interested audiences.
**Typical yield:** 5-20 targets per podcast/event.
**When to move on:** When you have covered the relevant podcast and event landscape.

## Stage 2: Engagement waterfall

Once a target is discovered and scored, engage using cascading depth. Start at the lightest touch and escalate based on response. Never skip steps unless the relationship already exists.

### 1. Comment on their content (value-add)

**What it is:** Leave thoughtful, substantive comments on their posts.

**Rules:**
- Comment must add value -- share an insight, ask a genuine question, add a data point, or offer a perspective
- Never comment "great post" or generic praise -- this is spam behavior
- Comment within 2 hours of their post for maximum visibility (algorithm boost)
- Aim for 2-4 sentences minimum
- Do not mention your product/service/brand in comments

**Frequency:** 2-3 comments per week on their content for 2-4 weeks before escalating.

**Success signal:** They like your comment, reply to your comment, or start recognizing your name.

### 2. Share/repost with commentary

**What it is:** Share their content to your audience with your own perspective added.

**Rules:**
- Always add 3+ sentences of your own perspective -- never empty reposts
- Credit them clearly (tag them)
- Explain why your audience should care about their content
- This signals to them that you are an amplifier, not a competitor

**Frequency:** 1-2 shares per week for priority targets.

**Success signal:** They engage with your share (like, comment, repost of your repost).

### 3. Direct engagement (reply, quote, mention)

**What it is:** Move from passive engagement to direct interaction.

**Rules:**
- Reply to their comments on others' posts (shows you follow their activity, not just their profile)
- Quote their content with a question or "building on this" framing
- Mention them in relevant posts (only when genuinely relevant -- never forced)
- Ask for their opinion on something in their expertise

**Frequency:** 1-2 direct interactions per week.

**Success signal:** They reply to you, follow you back, or initiate engagement with your content.

### 4. Collaboration (co-create, guest post)

**What it is:** Propose working together on content.

**Rules:**
- Only propose after at least 4 weeks of consistent engagement (steps 1-3)
- Lead with value to them, not what you get out of it
- Specific proposal beats vague "let's collaborate" -- suggest the topic, format, and timeline
- Start small: quote exchange, Twitter Space co-host, joint carousel
- Escalate to larger formats: podcast guest, joint webinar, co-authored article

**Collaboration formats by effort level:**
| Format | Effort | Reach multiplier | Best for |
|--------|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Quote exchange | Low | 1.5x | Initial collaboration, testing chemistry |
| Joint Twitter Space / LinkedIn Live | Medium | 2-3x | Building rapport, reaching both audiences live |
| Guest post / newsletter feature | Medium | 2-4x | Reaching their audience directly with your expertise |
| Co-created carousel / thread | Medium | 2-3x | Visual content collaboration with shared credit |
| Podcast guest appearance | High | 3-5x | Deep expertise showcase, long-form relationship |
| Joint webinar / course | High | 4-8x | Lead generation, authority building |

**Success signal:** They agree and promote the collaboration to their audience.

### 5. Community participation

**What it is:** Active contribution to community platforms where your target audience gathers.

**Rules:**
- Spend 2 weeks reading before posting -- understand community norms
- Answer questions before asking them
- Share resources and insights freely without promoting your brand
- Build a reputation as a helpful member before any self-promotion
- Follow community rules exactly -- one violation can permanently damage your reputation

**Participation model:**
| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1-2 | Read only. Understand norms, language, frequent topics. |
| 3-4 | Engage with existing threads. Answer questions. React to posts. |
| 5-6 | Start contributing original insights in relevant threads. |
| 7-8 | Share your content ONLY when directly relevant to a discussion. |
| 9+ | Become a recognized contributor. Others tag you in relevant discussions. |

**Success signal:** Community members tag you in relevant discussions. You get upvotes/reactions. Members follow you on other platforms.

### 6. Newsletter feature/swap

**What it is:** Cross-promote through newsletter audiences.

**Rules:**
- Only approach after establishing social relationship (steps 1-4)
- Offer to feature them first before asking for a feature
- Provide specific metrics (subscriber count, open rate, audience demographics)
- Match audience size and quality -- don't punch too far above or below

**Formats:**
- Recommendation swap: you recommend their newsletter, they recommend yours
- Guest section: write a section for their newsletter, they write for yours
- Interview feature: feature them with a Q&A, they do the same
- Beehiiv Boost: paid cross-promotion through the Beehiiv network

**Success signal:** Measurable subscriber growth from the swap. Continued relationship.

## Growth compounding

Each waterfall step feeds the next. This is how it compounds:

```
Discovery (find targets)
    |
    v
Comment engagement (build recognition)
    |
    v
Share/repost (they notice you amplify them)
    |
    v
Direct engagement (they engage back)
    |
    v
Collaboration (mutual content, shared audiences)
    |
    v
Their audience discovers you (new followers)
    |
    v
New followers engage with YOUR content
    |
    v
THEY become discoverable targets for others
    |
    v
Cycle repeats -- your audience becomes a discovery source
```

**Key metrics to track at each level:**
- Discovery: targets found per source, score distribution
- Comment engagement: reply rate from targets
- Share/repost: engagement rate on shared content vs original
- Direct engagement: follow-back rate, DM response rate
- Collaboration: audience growth from each collaboration
- Compound: percentage of new followers who came from existing follower engagement

## Platform-specific discovery tactics

### LinkedIn
- Use LinkedIn Search with filters: "Posts" + keyword + "Past week"
- Check "People also viewed" on relevant profiles
- Review LinkedIn newsletter recommendations
- Join LinkedIn Groups in your space (low competition, high targeting)
- Use Sales Navigator if available for advanced people search

### Twitter/X
- Create private Lists of targets to monitor without following
- Use advanced search operators: `keyword min_faves:50 -filter:replies`
- Monitor Twitter Spaces in your niche for active speakers and listeners
- Check "Topics" that are trending in your industry
- Review "Communities" tab for relevant groups

### Instagram
- Use Explore page and hashtag search for discovery
- Check Reels tab for trending creators in your space
- Review "Suggested for you" on profiles of targets you already follow
- Monitor Broadcast Channels of creators in your niche
- Check collaboration posts (tagged accounts) for network mapping

### TikTok
- Use search with niche keywords and filter by "Most liked" and "This week"
- Check "Suggested accounts" when viewing creator profiles
- Monitor trending sounds and hashtags in your niche
- Review duets and stitches for engaged creators
- Check comments for micro-creators engaging with larger accounts

### Reddit
- Use subreddit search and sort by "Rising" for fresh conversations
- Check user profiles of top contributors for other subreddits they are active in
- Use tools like Subreddit Stats to find related communities
- Monitor cross-posted content for community overlap signals

## Measurement framework

Track the following weekly:

| Metric | Source | Target |
|--------|--------|--------|
| New targets discovered | All discovery sources | 20-50 per week |
| Targets scored 51+ | Audience scoring | 30%+ of discovered |
| Comments placed (value-add) | Engagement tracking | 15-25 per week |
| Shares with commentary | Engagement tracking | 5-10 per week |
| Follow-backs from engaged targets | Platform analytics | 10-20% of actively engaged |
| Collaborations proposed | Outreach log | 2-3 per month |
| Collaborations completed | Outreach log | 1-2 per month |
| Follower growth from engagement | Platform analytics | Track source attribution |

## Anti-patterns (never do these)

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails | What to do instead |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Mass follow/unfollow | Platforms detect and penalize. Damages reputation. | Follow only targets you will genuinely engage with. |
| Generic comments at scale | "Nice post!" x100 is spam. People notice and block. | Write 10 thoughtful comments instead of 100 empty ones. |
| DM spam to new connections | Instant unfollow/block. Damages brand permanently. | Earn the DM through 4+ weeks of public engagement first. |
| Engagement pods | Fake signal. Algorithms are catching this. Real audience sees through it. | Build genuine engagement through quality content and real interaction. |
| Buying followers | Zero engagement. Ruins engagement rate. Obvious to anyone checking. | Grow organically. 1,000 real followers beat 100,000 fake ones. |
| Automating engagement | Generic automated comments are detectable and cringeworthy. | Manual engagement only. Quality over quantity. |
| Pitching in first interaction | Kills the relationship before it starts. | Provide value for weeks before mentioning anything promotional. |
| Engaging only when you need something | Transactional relationships do not compound. | Engage consistently regardless of whether you have something to promote. |

## Customization

**This is a default model.** Every workspace can override it.

To customize, add a `## Growth waterfall overrides` section to the workspace's RULES.md. Any overrides there take precedence over this default model.

Example override in RULES.md:
```
## Growth waterfall overrides
- Skip newsletter cross-promotion (we don't have a newsletter yet)
- Move community participation to step 2 (Reddit is our primary channel)
- Reduce comment engagement period from 4 weeks to 2 weeks before escalating
- Add custom source: "Investor portfolio companies" between step 4 and step 5
- Target 50+ discoveries per week (instead of 20-50)
```

If no overrides exist in the workspace RULES.md, use this default model as-is.
