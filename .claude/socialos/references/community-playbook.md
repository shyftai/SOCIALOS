# Community Engagement Playbook — SOCIAL:OS

Rules and strategies for community management across all platforms. Load before any engagement operation.

## Response Time Targets

| Priority | Target response time | Examples |
|----------|---------------------|----------|
| Critical | Within 30 minutes | Negative sentiment, PR crisis, account security issues, spam/bot attacks |
| High | Within 1 hour | Comments on own posts (first hour is critical for algorithm), direct questions, qualified leads in DMs |
| Medium | Within 4 hours | General DMs, mentions, tags, community questions |
| Low | Within 24 hours | General comments after first day, non-urgent DMs, forum posts |

| Platform | Comment response | DM response | Mention response |

## Engagement Types

### 1. Reactive Engagement
Responding to engagement on your own content.

**Comment responses:**
- Reply to every comment in the first hour after posting (algorithm boost)
- Reply to at least the first 10-15 comments on every post
- Ask follow-up questions to extend the conversation
- Pin the best comment (if the platform supports it)
- Like every comment, even if you don't reply

**DM responses:**
- Acknowledge within the target response time
- Answer questions directly -- don't redirect to a link unless necessary
- Never pitch in the first DM exchange
- If a DM is a sales opportunity, transition naturally -- don't switch to sales mode abruptly

**Mention/tag responses:**
- Thank genuine mentions
- Engage with the content that mentioned you (like, comment, share)
- If tagged in something irrelevant, ignore -- don't engage with bait

### 2. Proactive Engagement
Actively engaging with others' content to build relationships and visibility.

**See engagement-playbook.md for the full proactive engagement strategy.**

Summary:
- Daily engagement routine (15-30 min per platform)
- Comment on content from your target audience, peers, and industry leaders
- Meaningful comments only (5+ words, add perspective or ask a question)
- Track engagement activities for consistency

### 3. Community Building
Creating and nurturing a community around your brand or content.

**Principles:**
- Community > audience. Communities engage with each other, not just with you.
- Create opportunities for members to contribute (questions, polls, UGC)
- Celebrate community wins publicly
- Make community members feel seen (reply, feature, thank)
- Consistency is everything -- community dies when the creator goes silent

## DM Handling Rules

### Inbound DM categories

| Category | Action | Template |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Genuine question | Answer directly, be helpful | No template -- personalize |
| Sales opportunity | Acknowledge, answer question, suggest next step (call, resource) | "Great question. [Answer]. Happy to go deeper -- want to set up a quick call?" |
| Spam/bot | Ignore or block | N/A |
| Collaboration request | Evaluate fit, respond with interest level | "Thanks for reaching out. [Your assessment]. [Next step or pass]." |
| Complaint/negative | Acknowledge, empathize, offer solution | "I hear you. [Specific acknowledgment]. Here's what I can do: [solution]." |
| Pitch (someone selling to you) | Decline politely or ignore | "Appreciate the outreach. Not a fit right now." |

### DM rules
- Never pitch in the first DM. Ever. Build rapport first.
- Never send bulk DMs on any platform (ban risk).
- Personalize every DM -- reference something specific about the person.
- Voice notes on LinkedIn and Instagram DMs have higher response rates than text.
- Keep DMs concise. No essays. If the conversation needs depth, suggest a call.
- Never share sensitive information over DMs (use email for contracts, agreements).

## Crisis / Negative Comment Handling

### Severity levels

| Level | Definition | Response |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Minor | Single negative comment, disagreement, mild criticism | Acknowledge, respond constructively, move on |
| Moderate | Multiple negative comments, a small group upset | Respond publicly with empathy, take conversation to DM if needed |
| Severe | Viral negative attention, media pickup, mass complaints | Escalate to leadership, prepare official response, pause all scheduled content |

### Response framework for negative comments

**Step 1: Assess**
- Is this a legitimate complaint or a troll?
- Is this public or private?
- How many people are engaging with the negative comment?
- Could this escalate?

**Step 2: Respond (if legitimate)**
```
1. Acknowledge: "I hear you. [Specific thing they said]."
2. Empathize: "That's frustrating, and I understand why."
3. Address: "Here's what happened / Here's what we're doing about it."
4. Resolve: "I'd like to make this right. [Specific action]."
5. Move private: "Can I DM you to sort this out?" (if needed)
```

**Step 3: Handle trolls**
- Do NOT engage with bad-faith arguments
- Do NOT delete comments unless they violate community guidelines (deleting looks like censorship)
- If harassment: block, report, document
- If persistent: mute (they can still comment but you don't see it)

### What to NEVER do
- Argue publicly
- Delete legitimate criticism
- Respond emotionally or defensively
- Ignore a developing crisis
- Post new content during an active crisis without addressing it
- Use corporate PR language ("We value all our customers...")

## Community Platforms

### Reddit

**Engagement rules:**
- Lurk for 2+ weeks before posting in any subreddit
- Read and follow subreddit rules exactly
- 90/10 rule: 90% helpful comments, 10% your own content (maximum)
- Never post the same content in multiple subreddits simultaneously
- Respond to every comment on your posts
- Upvote good faith questions and contributions
- Give awards/upvotes to particularly helpful community members

**Building credibility:**
- Answer questions in your area of expertise
- Share original data and research
- Be transparent about affiliations
- Admit when you don't know something
- Build karma through genuine participation, not gaming

### Discord

**Engagement rules:**
- Introduce yourself in the intro channel
- Participate in conversations before sharing your content
- Follow channel-specific rules (some channels are promo-free)
- Be responsive to DMs from community members
- Use threads for extended discussions
- Help others -- Discord communities reward helpfulness

**Running your own Discord:**
- Keep channels focused (5-10 channels, not 50)
- Set clear rules and enforce them consistently
- Have moderators for communities >100 members
- Create regular events (AMAs, office hours, challenges)
- Archive dead channels -- don't let them clutter

### Slack Communities

**Engagement rules:**
- Same principles as Discord -- participate before promoting
- Share resources and tools, not self-promotional links
- Respond to questions in your area of expertise
- Don't DM members unsolicited (considered spam in most Slack communities)

## UGC (User-Generated Content) Management

### What is UGC
Content created by your audience about your brand, product, or content.

### How to encourage UGC
- Create a branded hashtag and ask people to use it
- Repost/reshare the best UGC (with credit and permission)
- Create challenges, prompts, or templates that people can customize
- Feature community members in your content
- Ask for testimonials, reviews, and case studies

### UGC rules
- Always credit the creator (tag them, mention them)
- Always get permission before reposting (DM or comment asking)
- Never edit UGC to change the meaning
- Feature diverse voices from your community
- Thank contributors publicly and privately

### UGC response template
```
"This is excellent -- love how you [specific thing they did].
Mind if I share this with our audience? Full credit to you."
```

## Influencer Engagement Rules

### Tiers

| Tier | Follower count | Engagement approach |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | Genuine engagement, DM for collaboration | Personal, peer-to-peer |
| Micro (10K-50K) | Strategic engagement, DM or email for partnerships | Semi-formal, value-exchange |
| Mid (50K-500K) | Strategic engagement, formal outreach | Professional, clear value prop |
| Macro (500K+) | Long-term engagement strategy, PR or agent contact | Formal, often through management |

### Engagement ladder
1. **Follow** their content. Genuinely consume it.
2. **Like** consistently (not every post -- that's creepy).
3. **Comment** with genuine, thoughtful responses (not "Great post!").
4. **Share** their content with your audience (with your own commentary).
5. **Reference** them in your content (quote, cite, recommend).
6. **DM/email** with a specific, mutually beneficial idea. Not a cold pitch.

### Rules
- Never engage with influencers solely for the purpose of getting them to engage back. It's transparent and off-putting.
- Provide value before asking for anything.
- If they don't respond, don't follow up more than once.
- Never pay for engagement (likes, comments). Pay for sponsored content if the partnership makes sense.
- Document all influencer relationships in `community/influencer-tracker.md`.
