# Social Media Writing Skill — SOCIAL:OS

Load this file before writing any social content. These principles govern all content creation across platforms.

---

## The Social Content Hierarchy

Every piece of social content follows this hierarchy. Never skip a level.

```
1. HOOK      — Stop the scroll. Earn the next 3 seconds.
2. VALUE     — Deliver on the hook's promise. Teach, inspire, or provoke.
3. CTA       — Tell them what to do next. One clear action.
```

**Hook** is non-negotiable. Without it, nothing else matters. The first line of every post, the first frame of every video, the first slide of every carousel — all hooks.

**Value** must match the hook. If you promise a framework, deliver one. If you ask a question, answer it. Bait-and-switch destroys trust.

**CTA** should be specific. "What do you think?" is weak. "Drop your biggest objection to X in the comments" is strong.

---

## Hook Formulas

Use these formulas. Rotate between them. Never start two consecutive posts with the same formula.

### 1. Contrarian Hook
Challenge conventional wisdom. Create tension.
```
"Most [role/people] think [common belief]. They're wrong."
"Stop [common practice]. Here's what actually works."
"[Popular advice] is terrible advice. Here's why."
"Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]."
```

### 2. Question Hook
Ask something the reader can't scroll past without thinking.
```
"Why do [x]% of [thing] fail?"
"What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"
"How many of these mistakes are you making?"
"What's the #1 reason [thing] fails? (It's not what you think.)"
```

### 3. Stat Hook
Lead with a surprising number. Numbers stop scrollers.
```
"[X]% of [audience] do [thing]. Only [Y]% succeed."
"I analyzed [N] [things]. Here's what the top [X]% do differently."
"[N] [things] in [time]. Here's exactly how."
```

### 4. Story Hook
Open with a specific moment. Drop the reader into a scene.
```
"Last [time period], I [specific thing that happened]."
"3 years ago, I was [bad situation]. Today, I [good situation]."
"My client called me at [time]. '[Exact quote.]'"
"I made a $[N] mistake. Here's the lesson."
```

### 5. Bold Claim Hook
Make a statement so strong the reader needs to see your evidence.
```
"[Outcome] in [time] without [expected effort]."
"The [thing] playbook is broken. Here's the replacement."
"This one change [result] in [time]."
"[Expert consensus] is dead. Here's what replaces it."
```

### 6. List/Number Hook
Promise a specific, countable set of insights.
```
"[N] [things] I wish I knew [time] ago."
"[N] [things] that separate [good] from [great]."
"[N] signs your [thing] is [problem]."
"The [N]-step framework for [result]."
```

## Platform-Specific Writing Rules

### LinkedIn

**Tone:** Professional but human. Conversational authority. Never corporate-speak.

**Structure rules:**
- First line is EVERYTHING. LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters with "...see more"
- Use line breaks liberally. One idea per line. White space is your friend.
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Use a pattern interrupt every 3-4 lines (question, bold statement, single-word line)
- End with a question or clear CTA to drive comments
- Tag people only when genuinely relevant (not for engagement farming)
- Hashtags go at the bottom, separated by a line break

**What works:**
- Personal stories with professional lessons
- Frameworks and mental models
- Behind-the-scenes and transparency posts
- "Here's what I learned" posts backed by data
- Carousel posts with actionable frameworks
- Polls (algorithm boost, but use sparingly)

**What to avoid:**
- "Thrilled to announce" openings
- Inspiration porn with no substance
- Agree/disagree polls with no depth
- Tagging 20 people for "thoughts?"
- Humble brags disguised as advice
- Posting about posting (meta-content about LinkedIn)

**LinkedIn carousel rules:**
- Slide 1: Hook + visual promise (what will they learn?)
- Slides 2-N: One point per slide, large text, minimal clutter
- Final slide: Summary + CTA (save, share, follow)
- Font: Large, readable. 24pt minimum for body text.
- Max 3 colors. Brand-consistent.
- Every slide must be readable in 3-5 seconds

**LinkedIn article rules:**
- Title: 40-49 characters performs best
- First 2-3 sentences visible in preview — must hook
- Use headers every 200-300 words
- Include 1-2 relevant images
- End with discussion question

**LinkedIn newsletter rules:**
- Subject line: Specific, benefit-driven, 5-9 words
- Open with the strongest insight, not context
- Keep to 500-1,000 words
- Use subheaders for scannability
- End with a clear takeaway and discussion prompt

### Twitter/X

**Tone:** Sharp, witty, opinionated. Economy of words is everything.

**Structure rules:**
- Every character counts. Cut ruthlessly.
- One idea per tweet. Never try to cover everything.
- Use line breaks for rhythm and readability
- Threads: First tweet is the hook. Last tweet is the CTA. Everything in between delivers value.
- No hashtags in the body of the tweet if possible (use 1-2 at the end if needed)

**What works:**
- Hot takes with substance
- "Here's the playbook" threads
- Quick tips and one-liners
- Reply-baiting questions
- Curated lists and recommendations
- Real-time commentary on industry events

**What to avoid:**
- Threads that could have been one tweet
- Self-promotional threads with no value
- Begging for retweets
- Overusing hashtags (more than 2 looks spammy)
- Quote-tweeting yourself repeatedly

**Thread writing rules:**
- Tweet 1: Hook that stands alone (must work even without the thread)
- Tweet 2: Context or "Here's what I mean"
- Tweets 3-N: One point per tweet, numbered if a list
- Final tweet: Summary + CTA + "Follow @handle for more on [topic]"
- Each tweet should be able to stand somewhat alone (people see individual tweets in timeline)
- Add a self-reply linking back to tweet 1 for discoverability

### Instagram

**Tone:** Visual-first, aspirational but authentic. Approachable expertise.

**Structure rules:**
- First line of caption must hook (shows in preview)
- Use line breaks and spacing for readability
- Hashtags in first comment OR at end of caption after 5 line breaks
- Mentions and tags should be genuine, not engagement-bait
- Geotags improve discovery for local content

**Feed post rules:**
- Image must stop the scroll independently of the caption
- Text on images: maximum 20% of the image area (for ad compatibility)
- Caption supports the visual, not the other way around

**Reel/Short rules:**
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds (text overlay + spoken word)
- Face on camera in first 3 seconds performs best
- Use trending audio when relevant (but don't force it)
- Caption/subtitle overlay for silent viewers
- 30-60 seconds is the sweet spot
- End with a CTA or loop back to the beginning

**Carousel rules:**
- Slide 1: Hook + topic promise
- Slide 2: Context or "why this matters"
- Slides 3-8: One point per slide
- Final slide: Summary + CTA (save this, share with someone who needs it)
- Consistent design across all slides
- Swipe indicator on slide 1 (arrow or "swipe" text)

**Story rules:**
- Use interactive stickers (polls, questions, quizzes) for engagement
- Keep text brief — stories are consumed fast
- Mix content types: text, photo, video, reposts
- Use story highlights to organize evergreen content

### TikTok

**Tone:** Raw, authentic, fast-paced. Anti-polished. Educational or entertaining.

**Script structure:**
```
[0-2 sec]  HOOK: Text overlay + spoken hook. Must create curiosity.
[2-15 sec] SETUP: Context. Why should they care?
[15-45 sec] VALUE: The actual insight, tip, or story.
[45-60 sec] CTA: What to do next. Follow, comment, save.
```

**Writing rules:**
- Write scripts as spoken word, not written word
- Short sentences. Conversational.
- Pattern interrupts every 5-7 seconds (cut, zoom, text change)
- Text overlays reinforce spoken words (not replace them)
- Caption should add context or include a CTA, not repeat the video
- Use trending sounds ONLY when they fit the content

**What works:**
- "Things I wish I knew" format
- Step-by-step tutorials
- Day-in-the-life with insights
- Myth-busting
- Before/after transformations
- Duets and stitches with commentary

**What to avoid:**
- Over-produced content (feels inauthentic)
- Direct self-promotion without value
- Trending sounds that don't match your content
- Ignoring comments (the algorithm watches engagement)

### Newsletter (Email)

**Tone:** Personal, direct, like writing to one smart friend. Not a broadcast.

**Structure rules:**
- Subject line: 5-9 words, specific, curiosity-driven or benefit-driven
- Preview text: Complement the subject line, don't repeat it
- Opening: Start with the strongest insight or a compelling hook
- Body: Use the 1-3-1 framework (see content-frameworks.md)
- CTA: One primary CTA, clear and specific
- Sign-off: Personal, brief, consistent

**What works:**
- "Here's what I'm seeing" curated insights
- Original frameworks and mental models
- Behind-the-scenes of real work
- Data breakdowns with interpretation
- Single-topic deep dives

**What to avoid:**
- "Happy Tuesday!" openings
- Apologizing for not sending recently
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Walls of unbroken text
- Sending without preview text

### Reddit

**Tone:** Genuine, community-first. Zero self-promotion smell. Match the subreddit's culture.

**Writing rules:**
- Read the subreddit rules before posting. Every time.
- Titles must be descriptive and honest. No clickbait.
- Provide value first, always. Self-promotion is earned.
- Respond to every comment on your posts
- Use Reddit-native formatting (headers, bullets, code blocks)
- Include a TL;DR for long posts

**What works:**
- Detailed how-tos with real examples
- AMAs with genuine expertise
- Data analysis and original research
- Honest failure stories with lessons
- Helping others in comments (builds karma and reputation)

**What to avoid:**
- Linking to your own content without context
- Using marketing speak
- Ignoring subreddit culture
- Posting the same content across multiple subreddits
- Deleting downvoted posts (own it, learn from it)

### Threads

**Tone:** Casual, conversational, community-oriented. More relaxed than Twitter.

**Writing rules:**
- Short, punchy posts work best
- Conversational and opinion-driven
- Engage in replies — the algorithm favors conversation
- Cross-post from Instagram stories for visual content
- Hashtags are optional and less important than other platforms

## CTA Patterns Per Platform

| Platform | Strong CTAs | Weak CTAs |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| LinkedIn | "What's your take on X?", "Save this for later.", "Tag someone who needs to see this." | "Thoughts?", "Like if you agree.", "Follow me." |
| Twitter/X | "RT if you've experienced this.", "Drop your best [X] below.", "Bookmark this thread." | "Please retweet.", "Follow for more.", "Like and share." |
| Instagram | "Save this for when you need it.", "Send this to someone who [X].", "Drop a [emoji] if [X]." | "Like this post.", "Follow for more.", "Link in bio." (overused) |
| TikTok | "Follow for part 2.", "Comment [X] and I'll send you [Y].", "Stitch this with your version." | "Like and follow.", "Share this video.", Generic calls. |
| YouTube | "Watch [related video] next.", "Subscribe if this was helpful.", "Drop your question in the comments." | "Smash that like button.", "Don't forget to subscribe.", Generic bells. |
| Newsletter | "Reply to this email with [X].", "Forward this to one person who [Y].", "Try this framework today." | "Click here.", "Check out my thing.", "Unsubscribe if..." |
| Reddit | "What's your experience with this?", "AMA in the comments.", "Here's the full breakdown." | "Check my profile.", "Visit my website.", Any self-promotion. |

## Hashtag Best Practices Per Platform

| Platform | Count | Placement | Strategy |
|----------|-------|-----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | 3-5 | Bottom of post, after line break | Mix: 1 branded + 2 industry + 1-2 niche |
| Twitter/X | 0-2 | End of tweet or woven into text | Only if adding discoverability. Less is more. |
| Instagram | 5-10 | First comment or end of caption | Mix: 2 broad + 3 mid-range + 3-5 niche |
| TikTok | 3-5 | In caption | Mix: 1-2 trending + 2-3 niche + 1 branded |
| YouTube | 3-5 | In description (first 3 appear above title) | Relevant, specific. Avoid generic tags. |
| Threads | 0-1 | In text, natural placement | Optional. Platform is hashtag-light. |
| Reddit | 0 | N/A | Hashtags are not used on Reddit. |

## Anti-Patterns

These destroy engagement, credibility, or both. Never do these.

### Wall of Text
Long, unbroken paragraphs with no visual breathing room. Especially deadly on mobile. Always use line breaks, short paragraphs, and visual structure.

### Clickbait Without Payoff
"You won't BELIEVE what happened next" followed by mediocre content. Hooks must be strong, but the content must deliver on the promise.

### Fake Engagement
Engagement pods, bought followers, comment-for-comment schemes. Algorithms detect these. Authentic engagement always wins long-term.

### Corporate Speak
"We're excited to announce our innovative solution that leverages cutting-edge technology to drive synergistic outcomes." Nobody reads past "we're excited to announce." Write like a human.

### Posting for Posting's Sake
Content without a purpose. Every post should either educate, entertain, inspire, or convert. If it doesn't do at least one, don't post it.

### Platform Copy-Paste
Taking a LinkedIn post and pasting it verbatim on Twitter, Instagram, and TikTok. Each platform has its own language, format, and expectations. Adapt, don't copy.

### Engagement Bait
"Like this if you breathe oxygen." "Comment YES if you agree." Empty engagement signals that add no value and make you look desperate.

### Self-Congratulation
"So proud to announce that I..." "Honored and humbled to..." Nobody cares about your feelings about your achievement. Share the insight, the lesson, or the value.

### Ignoring Comments
Posting and ghosting. The algorithm rewards creators who engage with their audience. Reply to comments, especially in the first hour.

### Over-Hashtagging
30 hashtags on Instagram, 10 on Twitter. It looks desperate and reduces readability. Use the recommended counts per platform.
