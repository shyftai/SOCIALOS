# Proactive Engagement Playbook — SOCIAL:OS

Strategy and execution framework for proactive engagement across platforms. Load before any engagement operation.

Proactive engagement = actively engaging with OTHER people's content to build visibility, relationships, and algorithmic favor. This is not about responding to your own comments (that's reactive -- see community-playbook.md).

---

## Why Proactive Engagement Matters

- Algorithms reward accounts that engage, not just accounts that post
- Commenting on a high-reach post puts your name in front of their audience
- Genuine engagement builds relationships that lead to collaboration, referrals, and opportunities
- Engaging before posting primes the algorithm to show your next post to more people
- It's the fastest way to grow on any platform as a new account

---

## Daily Engagement Routine

### Total time: 45-75 minutes per day (split across platforms)

| Block | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Morning (8-9 AM) | 20-30 min | LinkedIn engagement + Twitter/X engagement |
| Midday (12-1 PM) | 10-15 min | Instagram engagement + respond to AM comments |
| Afternoon (3-5 PM) | 15-30 min | Platform of focus + respond to midday comments |

### The 5-5-5 Daily Minimum

Every day, at minimum:
- **5 meaningful comments** on others' posts in your niche
- **5 likes/reactions** on relevant content
- **5 minutes** responding to your own comments

This is the floor, not the ceiling. Scale up as time allows.

## Comment Strategy

### On Your Own Posts

**First hour rule:** The first 60 minutes after posting are critical. Stay on the platform and reply to every comment. This signals to the algorithm that your post is generating conversation.

**Comment quality:**
- Ask follow-up questions to extend the thread
- Tag people whose expertise is relevant
- Add additional context or examples
- Thank insightful comments specifically ("Your point about X is exactly right because...")
- Pin the best comment (LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok)

**What NOT to do:**
- Reply with just "Thanks!" -- it adds nothing
- Reply to every comment with "Great point!" -- feels robotic
- Stop engaging after the first hour -- come back 2-3 times that day

### On Others' Posts

This is where proactive engagement happens. The goal: be noticed by the poster AND their audience by adding genuine value.

**The value-add comment formula:**
```
1. Acknowledge their point (briefly)
2. Add your own perspective, data, or experience
3. Optionally: ask a follow-up question
```

**Good comment examples:**
- "This matches what we saw -- [specific data point]. The one thing I'd add is [new angle]."
- "I tested this exact approach last quarter. Results were [specific outcome]. The tricky part is [nuance]."
- "Hard agree on point #2. I'd push back on point #4 though -- [respectful counterpoint with reasoning]."

**Bad comment examples:**
- "Great post!" (no value)
- "Love this!" (no value)
- "So true!" (no value)
- "Check out my [thing]" (self-promotion, never do this)
- "I wrote about this too -- [link]" (self-promotion disguised as engagement)

### Comment length guidelines

| Platform | Ideal comment length | Notes |
|----------|---------------------|-------|
| LinkedIn | 2-5 sentences | Longer, thoughtful comments are rewarded |
| Twitter/X | 1-3 sentences | Concise but substantive |
| Instagram | 1-3 sentences | Brief but genuine |
| TikTok | 1-2 sentences | Short, conversational, can be witty |
| YouTube | 2-4 sentences | Longer comments can become "top comment" |
| Reddit | 2-10 sentences | Depth is expected and rewarded |

## LinkedIn Engagement Warming

LinkedIn specifically rewards pre-posting engagement. Engaging with content 15-30 minutes before you publish your own post can increase your post's initial reach.

### The LinkedIn engagement sequence

**15-30 minutes before posting:**
1. Open LinkedIn feed
2. Like 5-10 posts from people in your network
3. Leave 3-5 substantive comments (see comment formula above)
4. Reply to any pending comments on your recent posts
5. View 5-10 profiles of people in your target audience (optional -- subtle signal)
6. NOW publish your post
7. Stay on the platform for 30-60 minutes engaging with comments on your new post

### Who to engage with on LinkedIn

| Priority | Who | Why |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Tier 1 | People who consistently engage with YOUR posts | Reciprocity. They're your core audience. |
| Tier 2 | Industry peers at your level or slightly above | Mutual growth. Their audience sees your comments. |
| Tier 3 | Target prospects / ideal clients | Visibility. They see your name before you ever pitch. |
| Tier 4 | Industry thought leaders with large audiences | Your comment is seen by thousands. High leverage. |
| Tier 5 | New connections (recently accepted) | Relationship building. First impression in the feed. |

### LinkedIn engagement limits (safety)

| Action | Daily safe limit | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Post likes | 50-100 | Beyond this, LinkedIn may flag as automated |
| Comments | 20-30 | Quality over quantity. More than 30 looks suspicious. |
| Connection requests | 20-25 | Stay well below 100/week |
| Profile views | 50-80 | Natural browsing pattern |
| Post shares | 5-10 | Don't over-reshare |

## Twitter/X Engagement Strategy

Twitter rewards real-time engagement and conversation. Being in the replies of popular tweets in your niche is one of the fastest growth strategies.

### Twitter engagement priorities

1. **Reply to tweets from accounts 2-10x your size.** Your reply is seen by their followers. If you add genuine value, you gain followers.
2. **Quote tweet with commentary.** Don't just retweet -- add your take. This creates original content while engaging with theirs.
3. **Join conversations early.** Getting in the replies within the first 30 minutes of a popular tweet gives your reply more visibility.
4. **Engage with your target audience's tweets.** Not just influencers -- engage with the people you want as followers/customers.

### Twitter engagement playbook

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Check trending topics in your niche. Reply to 3-5 relevant tweets. |
| Pre-post | Engage with 5-10 tweets (likes + replies) 15 min before publishing |
| Post-post | Stay in replies for 30 minutes. Respond to every reply on your tweet. |
| Afternoon | Quote tweet 1-2 interesting posts with your perspective |
| Evening | Like and bookmark 10-15 tweets for tomorrow's reference |

### Twitter engagement limits (safety)

| Action | Daily safe limit | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Tweets + replies | 50-100 | Combined. Don't exceed 200. |
| Likes | 100-200 | Beyond 300 risks rate limiting |
| Retweets | 20-30 | Quote tweets > plain retweets |
| Follows | 20-30 | Aggressive following triggers suspension |
| Unfollows | 20-30 | Mass unfollowing triggers suspension |

## Instagram Engagement Loops

Instagram's algorithm rewards accounts that spend time on the platform engaging, not just posting.

### The Instagram engagement loop

1. **Before posting:** Spend 10-15 minutes engaging with content in your niche hashtags and explore page
2. **After posting:** Respond to all comments within the first hour
3. **Story engagement:** Reply to 3-5 stories per day from people in your niche (DM replies to stories build relationships)
4. **Reel engagement:** Like and comment on 5-10 Reels in your niche daily
5. **Explore page seeding:** Engage with content similar to yours to train the algorithm

### Who to engage with on Instagram

| Target | Action | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Your commenters | Reply to every comment | Always |
| Niche peers | Like + comment on their posts | 5-10/day |
| Target audience | Like their posts, reply to stories | 5-10/day |
| Larger accounts in niche | Thoughtful comments on their Reels | 2-3/day |
| Hashtag communities | Engage with top posts under niche hashtags | 5-10/day |

### Instagram engagement limits (safety)

| Action | Hourly safe limit | Daily safe limit |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| Likes | 30-40 | 200-300 |
| Comments | 10-15 | 50-80 |
| Follows | 10-15 | 50-80 |
| DMs | 10-15 | 50-70 |
| Story views | No practical limit | No practical limit |

## Engagement Tracking and ROI

### What to track weekly

| Metric | How to measure | Target |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Comments written (on others' posts) | Manual count or tool tracking | 25-35/week minimum |
| Time spent engaging | Timer | 5-7 hours/week |
| Profile visits received | Platform analytics | Trending up week over week |
| Followers from engagement | Track new followers who you engaged with | 5-15/week |
| DMs initiated from engagement | Count organic DMs from people whose content you engaged with | 2-5/week |
| Collaborations sourced | Track opportunities from engagement | 1-2/month |

### Engagement-to-posting ratio

| Ratio | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|
| 1:1 (equal time posting and engaging) | Minimum viable. Most people don't even hit this. |
| 2:1 (2x engagement time vs. posting time) | Good. Growth accelerating. |
| 3:1 (3x engagement vs. posting) | Excellent for growth phase. Not sustainable forever. |
| 1:3 (posting 3x more than engaging) | Declining growth. Increase engagement. |

### ROI tracking template

```
## Engagement ROI -- {month}

Time invested: {n} hours
Posts commented on: {n}

Results:
- New followers attributed to engagement: {n}
- DMs received from engagement: {n}
- Collaborations initiated: {n}
- Profile visits trend: {up/down/flat}
- Own post engagement trend: {up/down/flat}

Top engagement wins:
1. {Commented on X's post about Y -- led to Z}
2. {Engaged with A's thread -- resulted in B}

Engagement quality score (self-assessment): {1-10}
Notes: {what to adjust next month}
```

## Platform-Specific Engagement Etiquette

### Do

| Platform | Etiquette |
|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | Thoughtful, professional comments. Reference their specific point. Add your experience. |
| Twitter/X | Quick, sharp, opinionated. Okay to be funny or contrarian. |
| Instagram | Genuine, warm. Compliment specifics. Ask real questions. |
| TikTok | Casual, conversational. Humor works. Short and punchy. |
| YouTube | Substantive. Reference specific timestamps. Add related insights. |
| Reddit | Deep, helpful, honest. Cite sources. Admit uncertainty. |

### Do NOT

| Platform | Avoid |
|----------|-------|
| All | "Great post!" with nothing else |
| All | Self-promotion in comments (unless explicitly invited) |
| All | Copy-pasting the same comment across posts |
| All | Engaging with content you haven't actually read/watched |
| LinkedIn | Over-tagging people who won't engage |
| Twitter/X | Reply-guying (inserting yourself into conversations you weren't part of with unwanted opinions) |
| Instagram | Follow-unfollow schemes |
| Reddit | Astroturfing or pretending to be a regular user while pushing an agenda |
