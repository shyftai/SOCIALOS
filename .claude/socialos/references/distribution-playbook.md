# Distribution Playbook — SOCIAL:OS

How to distribute content across platforms for maximum reach without looking like a spammer. Load before any cross-posting or repurposing operation.

---

## Core Principle

**Create once, adapt everywhere, copy-paste nowhere.**

Every platform has its own culture, format, and algorithm. Content that works on LinkedIn will fail on TikTok if posted verbatim. The goal is to extract the core idea and re-express it natively for each platform.

---

## Cross-Posting Rules

### What to ADAPT (rewrite for each platform)

| Content type | Adaptation required |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Text posts | Rewrite length, tone, structure, and CTA per platform |
| Video | Re-edit aspect ratio, pacing, captions, and hook per platform |
| Carousels | Redesign dimensions, slide count, and text density per platform |
| Threads | Restructure based on platform thread mechanics |
| CTAs | Change per platform (comment vs. save vs. DM vs. subscribe) |
| Hashtags | Different strategy per platform (see hashtag-strategy.md) |
| Links | Some platforms penalize links in-body -- adapt placement |

### What you can REPOST with minimal changes

| Content type | Repost OK? | Conditions |
|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Original images/graphics | Yes | If dimensions match the target platform |
| Short quotes/one-liners | Yes | If tone fits the platform |
| Data points/stats | Yes | Reformat the presentation |
| Video between Instagram Reels and TikTok | Caution | Remove platform watermarks. Algorithms detect and suppress cross-platform watermarks |
| Newsletter to LinkedIn article | Yes | With formatting adjustments |

### Never do this

- Post identical text across all platforms simultaneously
- Cross-post with another platform's watermark visible
- Use the same hashtag set across all platforms
- Include platform-specific CTAs on the wrong platform (e.g., "swipe" on Twitter)
- Share Instagram links on Twitter (preview doesn't render)

## Repurposing Waterfall

The core flow: long-form content flows down into shorter, platform-native formats.

```
LONG-FORM SOURCE
(blog, podcast, webinar, YouTube video, newsletter)
            |
            v
    PILLAR SOCIAL CONTENT
    (LinkedIn article, Twitter thread, YouTube standard)
            |
            v
    DERIVATIVE CONTENT
    (LinkedIn posts, tweets, Instagram carousels)
            |
            v
    MICRO CONTENT
    (Instagram stories, TikTok clips, Shorts, Threads posts)
            |
            v
    ENGAGEMENT CONTENT
    (polls, questions, quotes, hot takes derived from the source)
```

### Waterfall: From a Blog Post / Long Article

| Derivative | Platform | Adaptation |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Key insight post | LinkedIn | Extract the #1 insight, add personal angle, hook-value-CTA |
| Thread | Twitter/X | Break into 5-7 tweets, one idea per tweet |
| Carousel | LinkedIn, Instagram | Visualize the framework or steps, 8-10 slides |
| Short video | TikTok, Reels, Shorts | 30-60 sec talking head covering the main point |
| Quote graphic | Instagram, LinkedIn | Pull the strongest one-liner, design as image |
| Poll | LinkedIn, Twitter | Ask a question related to the article's thesis |
| Newsletter section | Email | Feature as a deep-dive section in the weekly send |
| Reddit post | Reddit | Rewrite as a genuine discussion post (no self-promo) |

### Waterfall: From a Podcast Episode

| Derivative | Platform | Adaptation |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Audiogram clip | Instagram, LinkedIn | 30-60 sec clip with waveform and captions |
| Key quote | Twitter, LinkedIn, Threads | Best quote as text post |
| Thread summary | Twitter | Summarize top 3-5 insights |
| Short video clip | TikTok, Reels, Shorts | Best 30-60 sec segment |
| Full episode | YouTube | Long-form video with chapters |
| Carousel | LinkedIn, Instagram | Key frameworks or takeaways |

### Waterfall: From a Webinar / Live Event

| Derivative | Platform | Adaptation |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Full recording | YouTube | With chapters and timestamps |
| Highlight clips | TikTok, Reels, Shorts | Best 3-5 moments, 30-60 sec each |
| Slide deck | LinkedIn carousel | Key slides redesigned for social |
| Recap thread | Twitter | Key takeaways, numbered |
| Behind-the-scenes | Instagram Stories | Setup, speakers, audience reactions |
| Follow-up post | LinkedIn | "3 things I learned from hosting [event]" |

## Platform-Native Adaptation Rules

### LinkedIn adaptation
- Longer form (800-1,300 chars). Professional but human tone.
- Add personal context or opinion. LinkedIn rewards personal perspective.
- End with a discussion question.
- No hashtags in the body. 3-5 at the bottom.

### Twitter/X adaptation
- Ruthlessly cut to the core idea. One tweet = one thought.
- For complex ideas, use threads. Hook tweet must stand alone.
- Opinionated and sharp. Twitter rewards bold takes.
- Minimal or no hashtags.

### Instagram adaptation
- Visual first. The image/video/carousel must work without the caption.
- Caption supports the visual, adds depth.
- Use Reels for reach, carousels for saves, stories for engagement.
- Hashtags in first comment or end of caption.

### TikTok adaptation
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds. Fast pace. Pattern interrupts every 5-7 sec.
- Talking head with text overlay is the standard educational format.
- Raw and authentic beats polished.
- Use trending sounds when they genuinely fit.

### YouTube adaptation
- Standard videos need strong thumbnails and titles (curiosity gap).
- Shorts must hook in first 2 seconds and loop well.
- Community posts for engagement between uploads.
- Descriptions should be SEO-optimized.

### Threads adaptation
- Short, conversational, opinion-driven.
- Casual tone. More relaxed than LinkedIn or Twitter.
- Engage in replies -- the algorithm is conversation-weighted.

### Reddit adaptation
- Value-first. Zero self-promotion smell.
- Match the subreddit's tone and norms.
- Provide genuine expertise in comments.
- Only link to your content if it's the best resource AND you've already established credibility.

### Newsletter adaptation
- Deeper context than any social post.
- Personal voice. Write like you're emailing one friend.
- Use the 1-3-1 framework.
- Link to your social content as "further reading" -- drive traffic back to social.

## Distribution Timeline

After publishing a piece of pillar content, distribute derivatives over time rather than all at once.

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Day 0 | Publish pillar content (blog, YouTube, newsletter) |
| Day 0 | LinkedIn post or article (first derivative) |
| Day 1 | Twitter thread |
| Day 1-2 | Instagram carousel or Reel |
| Day 2-3 | TikTok video |
| Day 3-4 | Threads post (shorter take) |
| Day 5-7 | Reddit post (if appropriate, with genuine value) |
| Day 7-14 | Repurpose into newsletter section if not already done |
| Day 14-30 | Reshare/repackage top performers with new angle |

## Employee Advocacy

Leveraging team members to amplify content. Only for companies/teams.

### Rules
- Never require employees to share -- provide, don't mandate
- Write suggested copy for each platform (they can customize)
- Personal accounts should feel personal, not corporate
- Stagger team posts over 2-4 hours (not all at once)
- Encourage employees to add their own perspective, not just reshare

### Suggested program structure
1. Share 3-5 pre-written post options per week
2. Include the "why" -- context on what the post supports
3. Provide platform-specific versions (LinkedIn, Twitter at minimum)
4. Track engagement to identify top advocates
5. Recognize and reward participation

## Community Seeding

Distributing content through communities (not just your own channels).

### Approved methods
- Share in relevant Slack communities where you're an active member
- Post in relevant Discord servers (follow their self-promo rules)
- Submit to relevant subreddits (follow 10% self-promotion rule)
- Share in industry newsletters via recommendation/cross-promo
- Post in relevant Facebook/LinkedIn groups (value-first, link second)

### Rules
- Only share in communities where you're an active, contributing member
- Always provide value in the community beyond just sharing your content
- Follow each community's self-promotion rules exactly
- Never share the same link in more than 3 communities
- Add unique context for each community -- explain why it's relevant to THEM

## Engagement Pods (with Caution)

Small groups of creators who agree to engage with each other's content.

### Acceptable
- Genuine engagement groups where members actually read and provide thoughtful comments
- Industry peer groups that share and discuss each other's work
- Accountability groups that hold each other to posting schedules

### Not acceptable
- "Like for like" groups with no genuine engagement
- Automated engagement tools
- Groups larger than 15-20 people (quality drops, algorithm detects patterns)
- Groups where engagement is forced or obligatory

### Best practice
If you participate in an engagement group, ensure your engagement is genuine. Write real comments. Only engage with content you actually find valuable. The algorithm can detect coordinated, shallow engagement.

## Paid Amplification Overview

When and how to boost social content with paid spend.

### When to boost
- Organic post reaches 2-3x your average within the first 2 hours (strong signal)
- Content directly supports a business goal (lead gen, launch, event)
- You're entering a new platform and need initial reach
- Content has high save/share rate but needs broader distribution

### When NOT to boost
- The organic engagement is weak (boosting bad content wastes money)
- You're boosting to hit a vanity metric
- The content doesn't have a clear CTA or next step for the reader
- You haven't tested the content organically first

### Budget guidelines
| Goal | Budget range | Platform | Expected CPM |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Awareness | $5-25/day | LinkedIn, Instagram | $8-30 |
| Engagement | $5-15/day | Instagram, TikTok | $5-15 |
| Website traffic | $10-50/day | LinkedIn, Meta | $10-40 |
| Lead generation | $20-100/day | LinkedIn, Meta | $20-80 |
| Newsletter growth | $5-20/day | Meta, Twitter | $3-10 per subscriber |

### Boosting checklist
- [ ] Post has proven organic engagement (above your average)
- [ ] Clear CTA aligned with business goal
- [ ] Target audience defined (not "everyone")
- [ ] Budget and duration set
- [ ] Tracking pixel/UTM parameters in place
- [ ] Landing page tested and working
