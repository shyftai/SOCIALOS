# Social Media Benchmarks — Reference

Use these benchmarks to evaluate social performance in health checks and reports.
These are cross-industry averages -- top performers exceed these. Niche, B2B, and B2C
benchmarks vary. Adjust expectations based on industry, audience size, and maturity.

Last updated: 2026-03

---

## LinkedIn

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| Engagement rate (per post) | <1% | 1-3% | 3-6% | >6% |
| Impressions per post | <500 | 500-2,000 | 2,000-10,000 | >10,000 |
| Follower growth rate (monthly) | <0.5% | 0.5-2% | 2-5% | >5% |
| Click-through rate (link posts) | <0.5% | 0.5-1.5% | 1.5-3% | >3% |
| Post reach (% of followers) | <5% | 5-15% | 15-30% | >30% |
| Carousel avg engagement | <2% | 2-5% | 5-10% | >10% |
| Article views per subscriber | <10% | 10-25% | 25-45% | >45% |
| Comment rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.5% | 0.5-2% | >2% |
| Save rate | <0.2% | 0.2-0.5% | 0.5-1.5% | >1.5% |

**Notes:**
- Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares + clicks) / impressions
- Carousel/document posts consistently outperform text-only posts by 2-3x on engagement
- Personal accounts typically see 5-10x the organic reach of company pages
- Posts with 3-5 hashtags get ~20% more reach than those with 0 or 10+

- Bookmark rate is a strong quality signal -- the algorithm weights it heavily

### YouTube Shorts

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| Views per Short | <500 | 500-5,000 | 5,000-50,000 | >50,000 |
| Engagement rate | <1% | 1-3% | 3-8% | >8% |

- YouTube Shorts success does NOT reliably translate to standard video success

| Bounce rate | >2% | 1-2% | 0.5-1% | <0.5% |

## How to Use These Benchmarks

1. **Health checks** -- Compare metrics against "Average" column. Flag anything in "Poor."
2. **Reports** -- Use to contextualize results. A 3% engagement rate on LinkedIn is "Good," not mediocre.
3. **Goal setting** -- Set targets at "Good" for new accounts, "Excellent" for mature ones.
4. **Troubleshooting** -- If a metric is "Poor," check the corresponding area:
   - Low reach --> content format, posting time, hashtag strategy
   - Low engagement --> hook quality, CTA, content relevance
   - Low follower growth --> profile optimization, engagement with others, content consistency
   - Low newsletter opens --> subject lines, send time, list hygiene
   - High unsubscribes --> content quality, frequency, audience fit
5. **Competitive context** -- Compare against competitor benchmarks, not just averages.
