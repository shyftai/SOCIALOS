# Content Frameworks — SOCIAL:OS

Reusable frameworks for social content creation. Load before creating content. Every framework includes the structure, when to use it, and platform fit.

---

## 1. Hook-Value-CTA (Universal Social Framework)

The foundational framework for all social content. Every post, thread, carousel, video, and newsletter follows this structure.

### Structure
```
HOOK    — 1-2 lines. Stop the scroll. Create curiosity or tension.
VALUE   — 3-10 lines. Deliver on the hook. Teach, reveal, or inspire.
CTA     — 1-2 lines. Direct the reader to one specific action.
```

### When to use
Every piece of social content. This is the default framework.

### Platform fit
All platforms. Adjust length and format per platform specs.

### Example (LinkedIn)
```
[HOOK]
Most founders write content that nobody reads.
It's not a writing problem. It's a structure problem.

[VALUE]
After studying 500+ high-performing LinkedIn posts, I found
the same 3-part pattern in all of them:

1. A hook that creates tension or curiosity in the first line
2. A body that delivers ONE clear insight (not five)
3. A CTA that gives the reader something specific to do

The posts that flopped? They buried the insight in paragraph 3
and ended with "thoughts?" as if that's a real ask.

[CTA]
What's the hook formula that works best for your audience?
Drop it below -- I'll share mine in the comments.
```

## 2. PAS for Social (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

Classic copywriting framework adapted for short-form social.

### Structure
```
PROBLEM   — Name the pain. Be specific. The reader should think "that's me."
AGITATE   — Twist the knife. Show the consequences of not solving it.
SOLUTION  — Present the answer. Make it feel achievable.
```

### When to use
- Product/service-related posts
- "Here's what I learned" posts
- Problem-solving content
- Newsletter sections that introduce a topic

### Platform fit
LinkedIn, Twitter threads, newsletter intros, Instagram carousels, TikTok scripts.

### Example (Twitter thread)
```
Tweet 1 [PROBLEM]:
Your content calendar is full but your engagement is flat.

You're posting 5x/week and getting 12 likes per post.

Something is broken. Here's what it is:

Tweet 2 [AGITATE]:
The problem isn't frequency. It's relevance.

You're creating content about what YOU want to say
instead of what your audience needs to hear.

Result: algorithmic irrelevance. Lower reach. Lower engagement. Repeat.

Tweet 3 [SOLUTION]:
Fix it with one question before every post:

"Would I stop scrolling for this if I didn't write it?"

If the answer is no, rewrite the hook or kill the post.

Your 5x/week should become 3x/week of content people actually want.
```

## 3. Hot Take Framework

Share a contrarian opinion, back it up, add nuance. High engagement potential.

### Structure
```
HOT TAKE    — Bold, contrarian statement. Challenges a norm.
EVIDENCE    — 2-3 supporting points. Data, experience, or logic.
NUANCE      — Acknowledge the other side. Show you're not just being provocative.
CTA         — Invite debate.
```

### When to use
- Thought leadership
- Industry commentary
- Differentiation from competitors
- Building a "point of view" brand

### Platform fit
LinkedIn (top performer), Twitter/X, Threads, Reddit (if genuine).

### Example (LinkedIn)
```
[HOT TAKE]
Content calendars are killing your content.

[EVIDENCE]
When you plan 30 days of content in advance, you optimize for
consistency at the expense of relevance.

The best-performing posts I've ever written were reactive --
responding to something that happened THIS week.

Data point: my planned posts average 2,400 impressions.
My reactive posts average 11,000.

[NUANCE]
I'm not saying don't plan. I'm saying leave 40% of your calendar
empty for real-time content. Plan the pillars. Leave room for the moments.

[CTA]
What % of your content is planned vs. reactive? Curious about the split.
```

## 4. Story Arc (for Threads and Carousels)

Narrative structure for multi-part content.

### Structure
```
SETUP       — Where were you? What was the situation?
TENSION     — What went wrong? What was the challenge?
TURNING POINT — What changed? What did you discover?
RESOLUTION  — What was the outcome?
LESSON      — What should the reader take away?
```

### When to use
- Personal stories with professional lessons
- Case studies
- "How I went from X to Y" content
- Carousel stories
- Video content

### Platform fit
LinkedIn threads/carousels, Twitter threads, Instagram carousels, TikTok, YouTube.

### Example (carousel structure)
```
Slide 1 [SETUP]:    "6 months ago, our social was dead."
Slide 2 [TENSION]:  "We were posting 5x/week. Getting 20 likes."
Slide 3 [TENSION]:  "We tried hashtag hacks. Posting time optimization. Nothing worked."
Slide 4 [TURNING POINT]: "Then we stopped posting about us and started posting about THEM."
Slide 5 [RESOLUTION]: "3 months later: 15x engagement, 3x followers, 40% of pipeline from social."
Slide 6 [LESSON]:   "The shift: from broadcasting to conversing."
Slide 7 [CTA]:      "Save this. Share with your team. Drop a question below."
```

## 5. The 1-3-1 Newsletter Framework

The default structure for newsletters. Simple, scannable, high-performing.

### Structure
```
1 — One strong opening (the hook / thesis / main insight)
3 — Three supporting sections (evidence, examples, frameworks)
1 — One clear CTA (what to do with this information)
```

### When to use
Every newsletter issue. This is the default newsletter format.

### Platform fit
Newsletter (Beehiiv, ConvertKit, Substack). Can also structure LinkedIn articles and long-form blog posts.

### Example
```
[1 - OPENING]
Subject: The 3 metrics that actually predict content success
Preview: Likes aren't one of them.

The most dangerous metric in social media is the one everyone obsesses over: likes.

Likes are vanity. They feel good and predict nothing.

After tracking 1,200 posts across 6 accounts, here are the 3 metrics
that actually correlate with business outcomes.

[3 - SECTIONS]

Section 1: Save Rate
Saves signal intent. When someone saves your post, they're telling the algorithm
"I need this later." A 2%+ save rate means you're creating reference-quality content.

Section 2: Share Rate
Shares are the highest form of endorsement. When someone shares your post,
they're staking their reputation on your content. Track shares-to-impressions, not raw count.

Section 3: DM Rate
The most underrated metric. When a post drives DMs, it means you've triggered
a private conversation. These convert to business at 10x the rate of public comments.

[1 - CTA]
This week, check your last 10 posts for save rate and share rate.
Reply to this email with what you find -- I'll send you a benchmark comparison.
```

## 6. Tutorial/How-To Framework

Step-by-step instructional content.

### Structure
```
PROMISE     — What will they be able to do after consuming this?
CONTEXT     — Why does this matter? Why now?
STEPS       — Numbered, specific steps. One action per step.
RESULT      — What the outcome looks like when done right.
CTA         — Try it and share your results.
```

### When to use
- Educational content
- Tool tutorials
- Process breakdowns
- "How I do X" posts

### Platform fit
All platforms. Carousels (LinkedIn, Instagram), threads (Twitter), videos (TikTok, YouTube), newsletters.

### Example (Instagram carousel)
```
Slide 1 [PROMISE]:  "How to write a LinkedIn hook in 60 seconds"
Slide 2 [CONTEXT]:  "Your first line determines if anyone reads the rest. 80% of readers never click 'see more.'"
Slide 3 [STEP 1]:   "Start with a specific number or bold claim."
Slide 4 [STEP 2]:   "Make it about the reader, not about you."
Slide 5 [STEP 3]:   "Create a gap between what they know and what you'll reveal."
Slide 6 [STEP 4]:   "Keep it under 15 words."
Slide 7 [RESULT]:   "Example: 'I analyzed 200 LinkedIn posts. Only 3% got this right.'"
Slide 8 [CTA]:      "Save this. Try it on your next post. Tag me -- I'll give you feedback."
```

## 7. Comparison Framework

Side-by-side analysis. High shareability.

### Structure
```
SETUP       — Introduce the two things being compared.
SIDE A      — Characteristics, pros/cons of option A.
SIDE B      — Characteristics, pros/cons of option B.
VERDICT     — Your recommendation (or "it depends" with clear criteria).
CTA         — Which do they prefer? Drive comments.
```

### When to use
- Tool comparisons
- Strategy comparisons
- "Old way vs. new way" posts
- Industry trend analysis

### Platform fit
Carousels (LinkedIn, Instagram), threads (Twitter), videos (TikTok, YouTube).

### Example (LinkedIn)
```
Content strategy in 2023 vs. 2026:

2023: Post 5x/week, optimize hashtags, grow followers.
2026: Post 3x/week, optimize for saves and shares, grow email list.

2023: Be on every platform.
2026: Own 1-2 platforms. Distribute to the rest.

2023: Engagement rate is king.
2026: Revenue from content is king.

The game changed. Most playbooks didn't.

Which shift hit hardest for you? Drop it below.
```

## 8. Before/After Framework

Transformation content. Shows the delta between states.

### Structure
```
BEFORE      — The old/bad state. Specific and relatable.
BRIDGE      — What changed (action, insight, decision).
AFTER       — The new/good state. Specific and measurable.
LESSON      — The principle behind the transformation.
```

### When to use
- Results showcases
- Personal growth stories
- Client case studies
- Process improvements

### Platform fit
All platforms. Especially effective for carousels and short-form video.

## 9. Day-in-the-Life Framework

Behind-the-scenes content. Builds trust through transparency.

### Structure
```
CONTEXT     — Who are you? What's your role?
TIMELINE    — Walk through key moments of the day.
INSIGHT     — What does this reveal about your work/industry?
TAKEAWAY    — What can the reader apply to their own life?
```

### When to use
- Personal brand building
- Team/company culture content
- Demystifying a role or industry
- Founder/operator content

### Platform fit
TikTok, Instagram (Reels/Stories), YouTube, LinkedIn.

---

## 10. Behind-the-Scenes Framework

Process transparency. Shows the work behind the result.

### Structure
```
THE RESULT  — Show the polished output.
THE PROCESS — Reveal the messy, real process behind it.
THE LESSON  — What does this teach about the craft?
THE INVITE  — Ask them to share their process.
```

### When to use
- Content creation process
- Product development
- Campaign strategy reveals
- Decision-making transparency

### Platform fit
All platforms. Video formats (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube) perform best.

## 11. Carousel Frameworks

### Problem-Solution Carousel
```
Slide 1: The Problem (hook)
Slide 2: Why it exists (context)
Slide 3: The Solution overview
Slides 4-7: Steps/details
Slide 8: Summary + CTA
```

### Listicle Carousel
```
Slide 1: "[N] [things] for [outcome]" (hook)
Slides 2-N: One item per slide with brief explanation
Final slide: Summary + CTA
```

### Story Carousel
```
Slide 1: The situation (hook)
Slides 2-3: The challenge
Slide 4: The turning point
Slides 5-6: The resolution
Slide 7: The lesson
Slide 8: CTA
```

### Framework/Model Carousel
```
Slide 1: Framework name + promise (hook)
Slide 2: Overview/diagram
Slides 3-7: One element per slide with explanation
Slide 8: How to apply it + CTA
```

### Data/Research Carousel
```
Slide 1: Key finding (hook)
Slide 2: Methodology (brief)
Slides 3-7: Data points with visualization
Slide 8: Implications + CTA
```

## Framework Selection Guide

| Content goal | Primary framework | Secondary framework |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Teach something | Tutorial/How-To | Carousel (Problem-Solution) |
| Share an opinion | Hot Take | PAS |
| Tell a story | Story Arc | Before/After |
| Compare options | Comparison | Carousel (Listicle) |
| Show results | Before/After | Story Arc |
| Build trust | Behind-the-Scenes | Day-in-the-Life |
| Drive engagement | Hot Take | Hook-Value-CTA |
| Newsletter issue | 1-3-1 | PAS |
| Video script | Hook-Value-CTA | Story Arc |
| Thread | Story Arc | Tutorial/How-To |
